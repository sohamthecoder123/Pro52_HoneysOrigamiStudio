Some Facts about Rabbits

  • Rabbits are cute, and are found in many places
  • They are also pretty soft
  • They are often confused with hares or jackrabbits. To identify a hare from a rabbit, take a look at the size. Hares are often larger than rabbits

Some Facts about Butterflies

  • Butterflies are beautiful insects, that are often found during the day.
  • They actually look very different from their larvae, which later undergo metamorphosis
  • Like rabbits, they are often confused with moths. The way to tell them apart is to see how they perch. Butterflies often perch with wings closed, while moths with wings open

Some Facts about Turtles

  • Turtlesa are amhibious reptiles, that spend most of their time underwater.
  • Turtles are slow on land, but fast in water. Some species reportedly even go 35 mph!
  • Often confused with tortoises, turtles have legs deigned for swimming, while tortoises for walking. There are some exceptions though, like the box turtle of North America.

Some Facts about Santa Claus

  • Santa Claus is a very famous figure associated with gift-giving during Christmas
  • He is a fictional character, but is inspired from a real life person, Saint Nicholas, who used to gift the poor and needy.
  • Santa is often depicted wearing a red-and-white suit, like in the design, but once upon a time he was actually associated with the colour green! It was only later that the colour changed to red
